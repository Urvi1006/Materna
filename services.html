Pelvic Floor Exercises

Pregnant women can take several measures to protect themselves and their developing baby Regular Prenatal Care: Attend all scheduled prenatal check-ups. This allows healthcare providers to monitor both the mother and baby's health, identify and address any potential issues early on. Healthy Diet: Maintain a balanced and nutritious diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, and dairy. Avoid raw or undercooked meats, fish, and eggs, as well as certain types of fish high in mercury. Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water to stay properly hydrated. Proper hydration is crucial for overall health, and it supports the increased blood volume during pregnancy. Exercise Safely: Engage in regular, low-impact exercises that are safe for pregnancy, such as walking, swimming, and prenatal yoga. Always consult with a healthcare provider before starting a new exercise routine. Avoid Harmful Substances: Stay away from alcohol, tobacco, and recreational drugs. Limit caffeine intake, and avoid exposure to harmful chemicals or toxins.
